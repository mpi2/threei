P15(PAF)/ mKIAA0101/ RIKEN cDNA 2810417H13

Mice at IMPC

Phenotype Summary:

Spleen - Increased conventional DCs low (CD11b low), decreased conventional DCs high (CD11b high), decreased plasmacytoid DCs, decreased CD5+ γδ T cells, decreased immature NK cells and increased mature NK cells
Mesenteric lymph nodes - Decreased total DCs
Bone Marrow - Decreased plasma cells, decreased pro B cells Hardy fraction A, and decreased CD3+ T cells
Peripheral blood leukocytes - Decreased CD4+ regulatory T cells

Gene information:

  • • p15(PAF) is a 12 kDa nuclear protein that acts as a regulator of DNA repair during DNA replication (see reference 1 below)
  • • genetic disruption of PAF by homologous recombination in mice results in altered hematopoietic stem cells function, disrupted progenitor development and leukopenia (see reference 2 below)
  • • overexpressed in several types of human cancer and is associated with poor prognosis (see reference 3 below)
